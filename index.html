Cross-Semester Reflection

Five Themes of Growth

01
From Intuition to Theory
I began designing by instinct. By PSet 6, every decision could be named and justified — cognitive load, productive failure, NOS, affordance — design choices backed by theory.
02
Affordance Thinking
One concept changed how I see all learning materials. Nine items can have far more than nine uses. I now ask: what affordances does each material have beyond its intended purpose?
03
Accountability and Stakes
Removing multiple-choice options (PSet 1), Commitment Cards (PSet 3), open-ended AI prompts (PSet 4) — the same principle throughout: learning deepens with real stakes.
04
Context as Curriculum
From Moon survival to YouTube editing to alien introductions — the best contexts are Trojan horses: familiar enough to engage, strange enough to teach. The learning hides inside the story.
05
From Adapting to Originating
PSets 1 and 3 adapted existing activities. PSets 4, 6, and 8 are wholly original. The trajectory is from improving other people's ideas to generating my own.
